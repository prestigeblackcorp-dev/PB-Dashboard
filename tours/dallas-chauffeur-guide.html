Stop-by-Stop · Logistics & Narration

The Playbook

Reunion Tower — GeO-Deck

300 Reunion Blvd E · Drop: GeO-Deck entrance off Reunion Blvd
Reunion Tower
Drop-offCurb at GeO-Deck entrance; help guests out curbside.
StagingHyatt Regency / Reunion lot or nearby curb; stay 3 min out.
RestroomInside, lobby & observation level.
Photo opPlaza base looking up; GeO-Deck for skyline shots.
TicketsPre-buy online; skip the line. ~$25/adult.
Budget45 min plenty; 60 if dining at Crown Block.
Welcome to the symbol of the Dallas skyline. That glowing sphere is the Reunion Tower — 561 feet tall, opened in 1978, and lit by hundreds of LED points that put on a show every night. We'll ride up to the GeO-Deck at 470 feet for a full 360 of the city before we go explore it street by street. Point out: the "Ball," the Hyatt's mirrored facade, downtown towers as you approach
Quick facts: Designed by Welton Becket · the geodesic dome has 259 lights · Crown Block restaurant sits inside the ball · best skyline orientation stop in the city.

Dallas World Aquarium

1801 N Griffin St · Drop: Griffin St main entrance, West End
World Aquarium
Drop-offGriffin St curb; tight street — drop fast, don't block.
StagingWest End lots; circle if needed. Confirm exact pickup time.
RestroomInside near entrance & café.
Photo opRainforest canopy, shark tunnel, manatee glass.
TicketsTimed-entry online recommended; busy weekends.
Budget60–90 min. Self-paced, one-way path top-to-bottom.
This one surprises everyone — it's not just tanks, it's a living rainforest in an old downtown warehouse. You'll walk down past free-flying toucans and a sloth, come face to face with jaguars, and end in a tunnel with sharks and rays swimming right over your head. Take your time — I'll be right here when you come out. Cue kids: "see if you can spot the sloth and the giant catfish"
Quick facts: Opened 1992 · the Orinoco & Mundo Maya exhibits are the highlights · cool indoors — a perfect midday heat-break.

Highland Park — Mansion Drive & Village

Highland Park Village
Lakeside Dr & Beverly Dr · Village: 47 Highland Park Village
RoutePreston Rd → Beverly → Lakeside Dr loop along the creek.
Drop-offHighland Park Village valet/curb for shopping stop.
StagingVillage lot; stay close for quick returns.
Photo opLakeside Dr fountains; Village Spanish architecture.
PaceDrive slow & narrate; offer 20-min browse.
EtiquetteResidential — no stopping on private streets, respect quiet.
We're entering Highland Park — one of the wealthiest neighborhoods in Texas, incorporated back in 1913. These estates run into the tens of millions. Up ahead is Highland Park Village: it opened in 1931 as the first planned shopping center in America, and today it's Chanel, Dior, Hermès, and Tom Ford under Spanish-Mediterranean arches. Say the word and I'll drop you to browse. Point out: Lakeside Drive fountains · old-money architecture · the Village tower
Quick facts: Highland Park Village is a National Historic Landmark · the Park Cities are "islands" within Dallas · nearby Preston Hollow is home to billionaires & a former U.S. president.

Dallas Zoo (Family Tour)

650 S R.L. Thornton Fwy · Drop: main gate, Oak Cliff
Dallas Zoo
Drop-offMain entrance plaza; big, easy turnaround.
StagingOn-site lot; longest stop — stage & reset cabin.
RestroomThroughout; note the front-gate ones for re-entry.
Photo opGiraffe feeding deck; the giant giraffe statue at the gate.
Budget1.5–2 hr. Suggest giraffe feeding early (sells out).
HeatPush water; ideal as the morning stop in summer.
This is the oldest and biggest zoo in Texas — over a hundred acres. The highlight is Giants of the Savanna, where elephants, giraffes, zebras and lions share one big habitat. Don't miss the giraffe feeding deck — you can hand them a snack right at eye level. That 67-foot giraffe statue out front? Tallest statue in Texas. Cue kids: "we'll get you up on the deck to feed a giraffe"
Quick facts: Founded 1888 · ~2,000 animals · Giants of the Savanna opened 2010 · monorail "Wilds of Africa" ride.

Deep Ellum & Arts District

Deep Ellum
Main/Elm/Commerce St (Deep Ellum) · Klyde Warren Park: 2012 Woodall Rodgers
RouteSlow loop Main → Elm for murals; then Arts District.
Drop-offKlyde Warren Park curb (Woodall Rodgers) for a stroll.
Photo opDeep Ellum murals; Nasher & DMA exteriors.
PaceMural cruise = no stop needed; park = 30-min walk.
Heat callIf brutal, keep it a narrated drive-through.
ParkingDeep Ellum streets tight; don't idle in bike lanes.
Welcome to Deep Ellum — the creative heart of Dallas. A hundred years ago this was the birthplace of Texas blues and jazz; today it's forty-plus live-music venues and over fifty murals splashed across these brick walls. Keep your camera ready — the art changes all the time. Then we'll roll into the Arts District, the largest in the country, past the Nasher and the Dallas Museum of Art. Point out: the biggest murals along Main & Elm · Klyde Warren Park built over the freeway
Quick facts: "Deep Ellum" = "Deep Elm" · Klyde Warren Park (2012) is a 5-acre deck over Woodall Rodgers Freeway · the Arts District holds the DMA (free), Nasher, Meyerson & Winspear.
FIFA World Cup 26™ · Operations

Match-Day Playbook

Dallas (AT&T Stadium = "Dallas Stadium") hosts 9 matches through July 14, plus a daily Fan Festival at Fair Park. Expect heavy closures, surge traffic, and excited guests. Know the schedule cold and pre-plan alternates.

  • Dallas Stadium: 5 group (Jun 14–27), 2 R32 (Jun 30 & Jul 3), R16 (Jul 6), Semi-Final (Jul 14)
  • Fan Festival: Fair Park, 3809 Grand Ave — free, daily Jun 11–Jul 19, up to 35,000 fans
  • Marquee group games include England v Croatia & Argentina fixtures

Routing Rules

  • Arlington (stadium) is ~28 min from downtown — budget double on match days.
  • Avoid I-30 near the stadium 3 hrs pre/post kickoff; use SH-360 / Division alternates.
  • Fair Park closures ripple onto I-30 & Exposition — route via Central (US-75) instead.
  • Pre-stage drop-off points; confirm rideshare/credential zones ahead.
  • Keep guests cool while waiting — long security lines in the heat.
Talking point: "Dallas is hosting more World Cup matches than any other city — nine, including a semi-final right here at AT&T Stadium. The free Fan Festival's at Fair Park if you want the atmosphere without a ticket."
When Plans Shift

Contingency Plays

Running behind

  • Convert Arts District walk → narrated drive-through.
  • Cut Pioneer/Dealey to a slow pass, no stop.
  • Protect the lunch reservation & the on-time home drop.

Extreme heat / storm

  • Favor indoor stops: Aquarium, Perot, NorthPark, Crown Block.
  • Mansion drive & Deep Ellum work great from the cool cabin.
  • Keep umbrellas ready; curbside drops only.

Closure / sold-out

  • Aquarium full → Perot or Klyde Warren food trucks.
  • Zoo line long → giraffe feeding may sell out; pivot to monorail.
  • Always carry a backup stop per leg.

Guests with kids / mobility

  • More restroom + snack buffers; shorter walks.
  • Confirm stroller/wheelchair access; drop closest to entrances.
  • Slow the narration; keep it interactive for kids.
The Prestige Black Standard

Service & Etiquette

Always

  • Greet by name; open every door.
  • Anticipate — water, climate, charger, music to taste.
  • Narrate with warmth, then read the room and give quiet.
  • Stage close; minimize guest time in the sun.
  • Drive smooth — gentle starts, stops, lane changes.
  • Confirm next pickup time & spot at every drop.
  • Spotless cabin; refresh between stops.
  • Discreet, professional, on-time to the minute.

Never

  • Rush, hover, or make guests wait curbside in heat.
  • Over-talk — let them experience the city.
  • Share other clients' info or personal opinions on politics.
  • Idle in bike lanes, crosswalks, or private drives.
  • Eat, vape, or take personal calls in front of guests.
  • Improvise pricing — defer to Prestige Black booking.
  • Leave the vehicle unsecured with belongings inside.

Gratuity: If asked, a customary chauffeur gratuity is ~20%. Never solicit; let service speak. Safety first — weather, water, seatbelts, and a calm, defensive drive always outrank the schedule.
