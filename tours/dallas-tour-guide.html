Iconic Skyline

Reunion Tower

The ball-topped silhouette that is Dallas. Ride to the GeO-Deck observation level, 470 feet up, for an unbroken 360° sweep — downtown's towers, the Trinity bridges, and the prairie horizon beyond. Interactive telescopes and HD zoom cameras bring the whole metroplex to your fingertips. It's the perfect opening act: see the entire city before you drive into it.

Time on site45 minutes
Best forAll guests · first stop
Up topCrown Block dining at the Cloud

02
Family Favorite

Dallas World Aquarium

Far more than an aquarium — a living rainforest in the heart of downtown's West End. Wind down past free-flying toucans and a three-toed sloth, eye-to-eye with jaguars and giant river otters, then descend into the Orinoco. Below, a walk-through tunnel puts sharks, rays, and sea turtles inches overhead, while manatees glide through emerald light. Compact, climate-cool, and endlessly photogenic — a guest favorite in any season, and a blessing on a hot Dallas afternoon.

Time on site60–90 minutes
Best forFamilies · couples · photos
Don't missThe Mundo Maya shark tunnel
Location1801 N Griffin St, Downtown

03
Texas-Sized Wild

Dallas Zoo

The oldest and largest zoo in Texas — 106 acres just south of downtown in Oak Cliff. The crown jewel is Giants of the Savanna, a sweeping habitat where elephants, giraffes, zebras, and lions share the horizon. Hand-feed a giraffe from the elevated deck, ride the Wilds of Africa adventure, and meet gorillas, koalas, and a 24-foot-tall giraffe statue that waves you in from the highway. Wide, stroller-easy paths make it effortless for every generation.

Time on site1.5–2 hours
Best forFamilies · kids
SignatureGiraffe feeding deck
Location650 S R.L. Thornton Fwy
Dallas Zoo
04
Science & Wonder

Perot Museum of Nature & Science

A striking cube floating above Victory Park, its glass-encased exterior escalator a landmark in itself. Inside, five floors of immersive halls: towering dinosaur skeletons, a hall of gems and minerals that glitters like a vault, an indoor tornado you can stand inside, and hands-on exhibits that thrill curious minds of every age. It's the intellectual heart of the Family Tour and a cool, contemplative break from the Texas sun.

Time on site1.5 hours
Best forFamilies · curious adults
Don't missThe Hall of Paleontology
Location2201 N Field St, Victory Park

Old-Money Dallas

Highland Park & the Mansion Drive

This is where Dallas keeps its fortune. Glide beneath canopy oaks along Lakeside Drive and Beverly, past manicured estates, fountained lawns, and the kind of architecture that whispers rather than shouts. We'll loop the Park Cities — Highland Park & University Park — and pause at Highland Park Village, America's first planned shopping center and now a Spanish-Mediterranean enclave of Chanel, Dior, Hermès, and Tom Ford. Step out to browse, or simply take it in from the comfort of your seat.

Two Sides of the City's Soul

Deep Ellum & White Rock Lake

Arts & Music

Deep Ellum

Dallas at its most electric. Just east of downtown, this historic district birthed Texas blues and now pulses with 40+ live-music venues, craft breweries, tattoo studios, and some of the most photographed street art in America — over 50 murals splash the brick walls. We cruise the neon-lit streets slowly so you can drink in the color, then step out for the famous wall-sized murals along Main and Elm.

VibeBohemian · creative
Best forMurals · photos · nightlife
Nature & Skyline

White Rock Lake

A 1,015-acre escape minutes from downtown, ringed by a scenic loop that delivers the city's best skyline-over-water view. Sailboats drift, herons wade, and the adjacent Dallas Arboretum & Botanical Garden — 66 acres of seasonal blooms on the eastern shore — ranks among the finest in the nation. A serene counterpoint to the city's energy, and a photographer's dream at golden hour.

VibeTranquil · scenic
Add-onDallas Arboretum gardens

Both pair beautifully with a sunset return. The Margaret Hunt Hill Bridge — Santiago Calatrava's soaring white arch — makes a stunning final photo on the way home.

Culture in the Core

The Arts District & Downtown Gems

A park built over a freeway.
Klyde Warren Park · the Arts District

Klyde Warren Park

A 5-acre deck-park built over a freeway — gourmet food trucks, lawns, and games linking Uptown to the Arts District.

Dallas Arts District

The nation's largest urban arts district: the free Dallas Museum of Art, the Nasher Sculpture Center, and the Meyerson & Winspear by Foster and Koolhaas.

The Sixth Floor & Dealey Plaza

Where history turned in 1963 — the JFK museum, the grassy knoll, and the X that marks the spot, told with quiet gravity.

Pioneer Plaza

49 larger-than-life bronze longhorns mid cattle-drive — the city's most beloved photo op, beside the historic Pioneer Cemetery.

Bishop Arts District

Oak Cliff's walkable village of indie boutiques, muralled alleys, and acclaimed kitchens — vintage charm with a modern palate.

Margaret Hunt Hill Bridge

Calatrava's 400-ft white arch over the Trinity — an architectural signature and a brilliant skyline framing device.

America's Stadium · FIFA World Cup 26™

AT&T Stadium & the World Cup

"Dallas Stadium" — host of 9 World Cup matches.
AT&T Stadium · Arlington, TX

Jerry World. The $1.3-billion home of the Dallas Cowboys, with a retractable roof, the largest video board in the NFL, and a serious contemporary art collection inside. For 2026 it becomes "Dallas Stadium," hosting nine FIFA World Cup matches — more than any other venue on the continent, culminating in a Semi-Final on July 14. We can route a stadium drive-by in Arlington, arrange a guided stadium-and-art tour, or build a full match-day experience around your tickets.

  • 5 group-stage matches (Jun 14–27) — incl. England v Croatia & Argentina
  • 2 Round-of-32 matches (Jun 30 & Jul 3)
  • Round-of-16 (Jul 6) · Semi-Final (Jul 14)
  • Free FIFA Fan Festival at Fair Park, daily through Jul 19

Day-Trip Extension

Fort Worth Stockyards

Fort Worth Stockyards

Where the West is still wild. Forty minutes west, the Stockyards National Historic District delivers the real Texas: a genuine cattle drive down Exchange Avenue twice daily (11:30 AM & 4:00 PM), where cowhands herd a fold of Texas Longhorns past the crowds. Add the world's largest honky-tonk at Billy Bob's Texas, championship rodeo, hand-tooled boots, and mesquite-smoked everything. A favorite half-day add-on for guests who want the cowboy soul beneath the city's gloss.
